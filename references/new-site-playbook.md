# New Site Playbook

Use this reference when the site is classified as `New site`.

## Core principle

Do not treat a new site like an established authority site.

A new site usually needs:
- clear commercial page foundations
- trust and proof assets
- a narrow content focus
- a realistic first 90-day sequence

A new site usually does not need:
- a large top-of-funnel publishing calendar from day one
- broad topic coverage across every adjacent niche
- a content program that outpaces site structure and conversion readiness

## New site diagnosis signals

Strong indicators:
- recent launch date provided by the user
- very small page inventory
- thin service, category, or product depth
- little visible internal linking structure
- weak evidence of existing rankings, trust, or authority
- very limited informational archive

If evidence is partial, label the conclusion as `likely new site` or `possible new site`.

## New site strategy model

Sequence recommendations in this order:
1. Build conversion-ready core pages.
2. Build essential trust pages and proof assets.
3. Create a narrow first cluster set around the strongest commercial or solution themes.
4. Expand into adjacent supporting topics only after the foundation is credible.
5. Review traction, then widen cluster breadth.

## Product-type guidance

### SaaS
Prioritize:
- homepage positioning clarity
- core feature pages
- top use-case pages
- alternatives or comparison footholds
- a small number of product-adjacent educational pieces

Avoid early overinvestment in:
- broad thought leadership topics with weak product linkage
- large template libraries before core pages are strong

### Ecommerce
Prioritize:
- clean category and collection structure
- high-value collection pages
- trust, shipping, returns, and product detail strength
- buying guides for the best commercial categories
- use-case support content that clearly routes to collections

Avoid early overinvestment in:
- broad editorial content that does not support categories
- too many low-value collection expansions before winners are identified

### B2B Service
Prioritize:
- core service pages
- industry or audience pages
- proof assets, process pages, and FAQs
- a tight set of problem-solution pages
- a few educational pages that feed service demand capture

Avoid early overinvestment in:
- broad educational traffic plays with no service bridge
- too many industry pages before the main service offer is clear

### Local Service
Prioritize:
- service pages
- service + location pages
- trust pages, reviews, and process explanation
- local FAQs and problem queries

Avoid early overinvestment in:
- non-local content expansion before local demand capture is covered

### Affiliate / Review
Prioritize:
- a narrow niche
- clear comparison logic
- evaluation criteria and trust signals
- first-wave best-of and versus pages

Avoid early overinvestment in:
- huge topical breadth without authority or testing depth

## What success looks like first

For new sites, early success often appears as:
- better indexing coverage
- initial keyword footprint growth
- more relevant impressions
- first rankings in narrow clusters
- first conversion-assisted pages becoming visible

Do not frame success only as immediate large traffic wins.
