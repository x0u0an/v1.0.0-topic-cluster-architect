# Strategy Framework

Use this reference to keep the skill's reasoning consistent and professional.

## Core operating model

The skill should not start from keywords alone.

Use this sequence:
1. Understand how the website makes money.
2. Identify the dominant page types and conversion path.
3. Classify the site archetype.
4. Assess site maturity and decide whether the site is new, early-stage, or established.
5. Infer the likely SEO growth objective.
6. Separate the topic universe by intent and business role.
7. Design clusters around acquisition, conversion support, and authority building.
8. Use competitor and inventory inputs only to refine prioritization.
9. Estimate likely SEO impact and success conditions.
10. Add a new-site strategy when relevant.
11. Deliver the result as a readable strategy report.


## Decision layers

### Layer 1: Business model
Ask:
- What is being sold?
- What action represents success?
- What page types likely matter most?

### Layer 2: Site archetype
Ask:
- Is this mainly SaaS, service, ecommerce, media, affiliate, marketplace, programmatic SEO, or hybrid?
- Is acquisition driven by editorial breadth, commercial pages, scalable landing pages, or a mix?

### Layer 3: Site maturity
Ask:
- Is this a new site, early-stage site, or established site?
- Does the site have enough page depth, trust, and topical footprint to support broad cluster expansion?
- Should the strategy prioritize foundation-building before authority expansion?

### Layer 4: Intent system
Separate:
- demand capture
- demand generation
- education
- comparison
- use-case discovery
- trust or proof support

### Layer 5: Architecture
Build:
- top-level cluster groups
- hub or pillar pages
- supporting pages
- internal linking logic
- monetization or conversion adjacency

### Layer 6: Prioritization
Sequence by:
- revenue adjacency
- strength of user intent
- speed to publish
- leverage on existing assets
- defensibility over time
- maturity fit for the current site stage

### Layer 7: Expected impact and feasibility
State:
- likely SEO impact level
- what conditions must be true for results
- what constraints may slow or limit outcomes
- what early success should look like, especially for new sites


## What makes this skill valuable

This skill adds value because it combines:
- diagnosis of website type
- diagnosis of site maturity and new-site constraints
- strategy tailored to business model
- cluster design instead of keyword dumping
- optional competitive and content-inventory analysis
- realistic impact framing instead of overpromising
- readable report delivery for non-experts


## Failure modes to avoid

Avoid outputs that:
- jump into topic ideas before identifying the site type
- ignore whether the site is new or mature
- recommend blog-heavy programs for obviously commercial sites without a conversion bridge
- give a broad authority strategy to a new site that lacks foundation pages
- copy competitor topics without filtering for fit
- ignore existing strong pages or clusters
- produce long walls of text that are hard to use
