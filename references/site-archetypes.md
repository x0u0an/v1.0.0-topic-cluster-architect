# Site Archetypes Reference

Use this reference to diagnose the site's business model before designing clusters.

## SaaS

### Common signals
- Product-led navigation
- Feature pages, pricing, integrations, templates, docs, help center
- Demo, free trial, or signup as the main conversion

### Cluster emphasis
- Use cases
- Jobs-to-be-done
- Feature education
- Integrations
- Alternative and comparison pages
- Persona or vertical solution pages

### Common mistakes
- Overinvesting in top-of-funnel topics with weak product connection
- Ignoring BOFU comparison and alternative content

## B2B Service

### Common signals
- Service pages, industries served, case studies, consultation CTA
- Sales-led conversion path
- Expertise and trust are central to conversion

### Cluster emphasis
- Service clusters
- Problem-solution clusters
- Industry-specific clusters
- Process, methodology, and case-study support
- Objection-handling content

### Common mistakes
- Writing broad blog content with no path back to services
- Underbuilding industry and use-case depth

## Local Service

### Common signals
- Location pages
- Phone call, quote, appointment, or visit as the main conversion
- Strong geographic intent in queries

### Cluster emphasis
- Service + location combinations
- Local problem queries
- Trust, proof, reviews, and process content
- FAQ content tied to local buyer concerns

### Common mistakes
- Building national-style topic clusters without local intent
- Ignoring city, neighborhood, or service-area modifiers

## Ecommerce

### Common signals
- Product catalog, collection pages, filters, cart, checkout
- Revenue mainly from product sales
- Category hierarchy matters more than blog volume alone

### Cluster emphasis
- Category support content
- Buying guides
- Comparisons and best-for pages
- Use-case education
- Care, maintenance, sizing, compatibility, accessory, and bundle content

### Common mistakes
- Treating blog posts as the main authority engine while category pages remain weak
- Publishing informational content with no category or product bridge

## Media / Blog

### Common signals
- Editorial publishing cadence
- Large article library
- Ad, sponsorship, subscription, or authority model
- Content is the core product or growth engine

### Cluster emphasis
- Topic breadth and depth
- Editorial series
- Clear hub-and-subtopic ownership
- Content refresh and pruning logic

### Common mistakes
- Overcompressing into only commercial clusters
- Weak topic governance across the archive

## Affiliate / Review

### Common signals
- Review, comparison, and buyer-intent content dominate
- Monetization comes from affiliate clicks or referral actions
- Product testing, lists, and verdict content are central

### Cluster emphasis
- Best-of pages
- Versus pages
- Alternative pages
- Use-case decision content
- Buyer guides and category comparisons

### Common mistakes
- Overrelying on broad informational traffic without commercial mapping
- Thin comparison frameworks and weak differentiation logic

## Marketplace / Directory

### Common signals
- Listings, providers, vendors, jobs, tools, venues, or directories
- Demand and supply entities coexist
- Search often depends on location, category, attribute, or intent filters

### Cluster emphasis
- Category hubs
- Location hubs
- Entity-specific landing pages
- Comparison or decision support content
- Query patterns that connect users to listings

### Common mistakes
- Designing blog-only strategies for a listing-driven site
- Ignoring scalable landing page patterns

## Programmatic SEO

### Common signals
- Large sets of similar landing pages driven by entities, templates, or combinations
- Structured data model behind content generation
- Growth relies on repeatable page logic

### Cluster emphasis
- Template families
- Entity segmentation
- Supporting editorial pages that strengthen template pages
- Internal linking between head terms and generated long-tail pages

### Common mistakes
- Treating every page as bespoke editorial content
- Shipping page templates without unique value or navigational logic

## Hybrid

### Common signals
- Multiple growth models coexist, such as SaaS plus template library or ecommerce plus editorial hub
- More than one page family contributes meaningfully to acquisition or conversion

### Cluster emphasis
- Identify the primary acquisition engine
- Identify the supporting conversion engine
- Design clusters around both, but do not split focus equally unless justified

### Common mistakes
- Forcing a single-model strategy onto a mixed site
- Failing to explain the role of each content layer

## Diagnosis Heuristics

When classification is unclear, inspect:
- the main CTA above the fold
- the navigation and page hierarchy
- the ratio of commercial pages to editorial pages
- how the site appears to make money
- whether SEO likely supports demand capture, authority building, or scaled landing pages

If evidence remains mixed, output:
- Primary archetype
- Secondary archetype
- Why the hybrid matters for content architecture
